## Brand & Style

This design system embodies the intersection of high-level strategic intelligence and human-centric warmth. It is inspired by the architectural precision of contemporary design and the understated luxury of Aman Resorts. The visual language avoids the "tech-first" tropes of the AI industry, opting instead for a "consultancy-first" approach that prioritizes legacy, discretion, and profound clarity.

The style is **Minimalist Editorial**. It leverages vast whitespace to represent clarity of thought and the "calm" that effective communication systems bring to a business. Every element is intentional, avoiding decorative flourishes in favor of structural beauty and tactile elegance. The focus is squarely on "tu negocio" (your business), positioning the technology as a silent, powerful partner in human success.

## Colors

The palette is rooted in natural, high-end materials: graphite, ivory, walnut, and bronze.

- **Ivory (#F6F4EF):** The primary canvas. It provides a warmer, more human experience than pure white, reminiscent of high-quality paper or polished stone.
- **Graphite Black (#0E0E10):** Used for typography and primary structural elements. It provides a deep, authoritative contrast without being as harsh as absolute black.
- **Bronze (#D4A54A):** A sophisticated accent used sparingly for calls to action, highlights, and status indicators. It should feel like a metallic inlay in architecture.
- **Stone Grey (#706F6C):** A secondary neutral used for supportive text and subtle dividers to maintain hierarchy without breaking the editorial flow.

## Typography

**Manrope** is used throughout to maintain a modern, technical yet approachable feel. The hierarchy relies on extreme scale and weight shifts rather than multiple typefaces.

- **Display & Headlines:** Use light weights (300-400) for large sizes to evoke elegance. Tighten letter-spacing as the size increases to maintain a cohesive, "designed" look.
- **Body Text:** Set with generous line-height (1.6x) to ensure an effortless reading experience.
- **Labels:** Small, uppercase labels with increased tracking (letter-spacing) are used for metadata, navigation, and accent headers, mimicking architectural labeling.
- **Emphasis:** Use the Bronze accent color for key phrases within body text to draw the eye without disrupting the reading rhythm.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout is governed by a **Fixed Grid** system that prioritizes generous margins and a sense of "the center." 

- **Grid:** A 12-column grid for desktop, a 6-column grid for tablet, and a 2-column grid for mobile.
- **The "Breathe" Principle:** Section gaps are intentionally large (160px+) to ensure that distinct ideas do not bleed into one another, allowing the user to process "tu negocio" in stages.
- **Alignment:** Prefer left-aligned typography for editorial clarity, with occasional centered display text for high-impact landing moments.
- **Margins:** Desktop margins are significant (80px) to frame the content like a luxury publication.

## Elevation & Depth

In this design system, depth is achieved through **Tonal Layers** and texture rather than heavy shadows.

- **Surfaces:** Use subtle shifts in the neutral palette (e.g., a slightly darker Ivory vs. the base Ivory) to distinguish between the background and a container.
- **Hairline Outlines:** 1px borders in Stone Grey or low-opacity Graphite are preferred over shadows to define interactive areas.
- **The "Glass" Effect:** For floating elements or navigation menus, use a high-blur backdrop filter with a semi-transparent Ivory tint, creating the effect of frosted glass—clean, modern, and high-end.
- **Micro-Shadows:** If a shadow is necessary for accessibility, use a very diffused, low-opacity "ambient" shadow with a slight Bronze tint to maintain the warm aesthetic.

## Shapes

The shape language is **Soft (0.25rem / 4px)**. This mimics the precision-milled edges of a Porsche dashboard or the corner of a marble slab. 

- **Sharpness for Authority:** We avoid overly rounded or "bubbly" corners to maintain a professional, sober tone.
- **Functional Shapes:** Elements like inputs and cards use the base 4px radius. 
- **The Exception:** Circular "pill" shapes are reserved exclusively for tags or status chips to provide a clear visual departure from structural components.

## Components

### Buttons
Primary buttons are Graphite with Ivory text. They should feel substantial but minimal. Hover states should subtly transition to a Bronze border or text color. Secondary buttons use a simple hairline border.

### Cards
Cards are defined by hairline borders or subtle tonal shifts rather than shadows. They should have generous internal padding (at least 32px) to prevent content from feeling cramped.

### Input Fields
Inputs are minimalist: a single bottom border or a very light-toned Ivory background. Focus states are marked by a transition to Bronze for the underline or border, emphasizing the "warm light" metaphor.

### Chips & Tags
Used for categorizing "tu negocio" attributes. Use the Ivory base with a Stone Grey hairline border and Label-Caps typography.

### Imagery
Use high-resolution photography featuring contemporary architecture, natural textures (walnut grain, marble veins), and human interaction in warm, natural light. Avoid literal "AI" imagery like glowing brains or blue circuitry.
